DPGMM MODEL SPECIFICATION
--------------------------
  Class:         sklearn.mixture.BayesianGaussianMixture (sklearn v1.8.0)
  n_components:  10 (upper bound; effective k determined by data)
  covariance_type: full
  weight_concentration_prior_type: dirichlet_process
  n_init:        5
  max_iter:      500
  random_state:  42
  Converged:     True
  Lower bound:   19048.1830

  SAMPLE WEIGHT NOTE: BayesianGaussianMixture.fit() does not accept
  sample_weight in any sklearn version (including 1.8.0). The model was fit
  unweighted on standardized factor scores. This is defensible because:
  (1) Factor scores were derived from a weighted Thomson regression on a
      weighted polychoric matrix, so weights are embedded in the score space.
  (2) commonpostweight is applied to ALL post-fit reporting (cluster sizes,
      factor score means, cross-tabs, item means). All reported statistics
      are population-representative.

KEY SUBSTANTIVE OBSERVATIONS (pre-naming)
------------------------------------------
  These are structural patterns for the analyst to consider — not cluster names.

  1. The election distrust axis (F2) creates near-perfect partisan mirror pairs:
       C0 (Republican, trust elections) ↔ C6 (Republican, trust elections) — both F2-low
       C3 (Republican, distrust)        ↔ C5 (Republican, distrust) — both F2-high
       C1 (Democrat, trust)             ↔ C4 (Democrat, trust) — both F2-low
       C2 (Democrat/cross-partisan, distrust) — Democratic-leaning but election-skeptical
     Within each "trust/distrust" pair, the distinguishing dimension is F5 (values conservatism).

  2. C3 is the most extreme cluster on F5r (values conservatism = +1.09), most
     socially conservative (79% Con/VCon, 41% VCon, 60% Republican, 1.2% Democrat),
     highest income (17.9 on 1–16 scale), highest born-again rate (47.7%), and
     ALSO distrusts elections (F2=+0.43). Furthest from C8 and C9 (Mahal. dist. 3.17).

  3. C7 is structurally anomalous: high F1 (enforcement, +0.72) combined with
     extreme low F5r (values = −1.09), 45% Democrat, 48% Moderate, and F2-negative
     (trusts elections). Also the oldest cluster (40% retired, median age implied high).
     This is not simply "hawk Democrat" — the very low values conservatism alongside
     high enforcement is an unusual combination requiring careful labeling.

  4. C8 and C9 are the liberal-secular poles (C8: 55% VLib, 5.8% non-binary, 62% Dem,
     very young — only 5.7% retired, 15% unemployed; C9: 75% Dem, 51% VLib, highly
     educated, high income, 25% Northeast). Both have near-zero abortion restriction
     scores (12.4 / 9.5 on the 40-pt scale). They differ primarily on F2 (C8 distrusts
     elections; C9 trusts) and demographics (C8 younger/lower-income/more diverse;
     C9 older/more educated/higher-income/whiter/more Northeast).

  5. Government trust (F3) is nearly flat across all clusters (range ~0.50 on a
     standardized scale), confirming its near-orthogonality to the partisan structure.
     The govt_trust_imputed flag concentrates most heavily in C3 (42.8%) and C5 (37.0%)
     — the two Republican-leaning election-distrust clusters. These clusters have the
     highest rates of "Not Sure" on govt trust items, suggesting genuine ambivalence
     about institutional trust (or strategic evasion).

  6. Cluster 2 has markedly elevated racial diversity (27.3% Black, 15.1% Hispanic,
     vs. 11.6% / 9.6% overall), high born-again rate (37.7%), moderate ideology
     (42% Moderate), and high election distrust (F2=+0.66). It straddles partisan
     lines (36% Dem, 17% Rep, 29% Ind, 18% Other). This is the most compositionally
     heterogeneous cluster and its election skepticism appears to have a different
     character than the Republican-leaning C3/C5 election distrust.

TECHNICAL NOTES
---------------
  1. BayesianGaussianMixture sample_weight limitation (sklearn v1.8.0):
     fit() does not accept sample_weight. Fitting is unweighted.
     See dpgmm_clustering.py line 64–70 for full rationale.
     All reporting uses commonpostweight.

  2. Row alignment between efa_factor_scores.csv and cluster assignments:
     Verified via commonpostweight comparison (max abs diff = 8.85e-17).
     Both files are derived from the same DTA read + listwise deletion in
     the same order. Row index is 1:1.

  3. Proportion variables in cluster_item_means.csv are stored as 0–100
     (percentage points), not 0–1. The ×100 conversion was applied in
     cluster_item_profiles.py after wmean() on 0/1 binary indicators.
     This was corrected from an initial 0–1 display bug.

  4. CC24_325 (abortion weeks): encoded as 40−raw in EFA recoding.
     A mean of 23.9 overall ≈ respondent supports allowing abortion up to
     ~16 weeks (40−23.9=16.1). C9 mean of 9.5 ≈ ~30 weeks; C3 mean of
     32.1 ≈ ~8 weeks or near-total restriction.

  5. pew_bornagain coding verified: 1=Yes (born-again), 2=No.
     Weighted % yes = 32.6% in full sample; 31.3% in analytic subsample.
     Confirmed correct.

  6. Cluster label ordering: labels 0–9 are sorted by DESCENDING weighted N
     (C0 = largest cluster). This remapping was applied after DPGMM prediction.
